Carbon Footprint 101

A **carbon footprint** represents the total greenhouse gases (CO2, methane, nitrous oxides) emitted by our actions. It is expressed in **CO2 equivalent (CO2e)** to standardize atmospheric heating impacts.

To keep warming under 1.5°C, the global average footprint per human must fall to **under 2,000 kg CO2e / year** by 2050. Currently, regional averages vary from 16,000 kg in heavy industrialized areas to under 1,000 kg in developing economies.

Direct Reductions vs Offsets

Carbon offsetting (e.g. tree plantations, solar farms) should be used as a last resort. Direct reductions are verified, permanent, and do not suffer from lag times or audit failures.

"Reducing your gross output by adjustments in diet, travel, and heating efficiency holds double the climate efficiency of secondary carbon offsets."

Focus on tracking and completing goals inside the **Reduce** panel before purchasing carbon offset shares.
